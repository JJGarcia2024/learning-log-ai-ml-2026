# Charlie Munger's Investment Hierarchy
*Date: June 16, 2026*

Based on Charlie Munger's lifelong evolution as an investor (and the exact way he convinced Warren Buffett to change Berkshire Hathaway's strategy), here is the strict hierarchy of how you should prioritize buying companies. 

The algorithm we built maps perfectly to this hierarchy!

### 🥇 Tier 1: The "Hidden Gem" (Wonderful Business at a Wonderful Price)
*   **What it is:** The absolute holy grail of investing. This is a company with a massive, durable competitive advantage (moat), huge pricing power, and high returns on capital—but due to temporary market panic, obscurity, or a short-term solvable problem, it is trading at a massive discount. 
*   **The Munger View:** When you find one of these, Munger says you should "back up the truck" and bet heavily. 
*   **In our project:** These are the **86 stocks** in the *Sustainable Hidden Gems* list (High Yield + High Margin + Low Debt). 

### 🥈 Tier 2: The "Munger Premium" (A Great Business at a Fair Price)
*   **What it is:** This is the tier Munger is most famous for. These are elite compounders (like See's Candies, Apple, or Costco) with dominant monopolies and $\ge$ 20% ROEs. The catch? The market *knows* they are great, so they are rarely "cheap." You have to pay a premium.
*   **The Munger View:** Munger completely revolutionized value investing by proving that it is mathematically better to pay a premium price for a great business than a cheap price for a bad one. As long as the price is "fair," the internal compounding engine of the business will make you rich over 10-20 years. 
*   **In our project:** These are the **48 stocks** in the *Great Businesses at a Fair Price* list (Lower Yield, but Elite ROE & Margins).

### 🥉 Tier 3: The "Cigar Butt" (A Fair/Lousy Business at a Great Price)
*   **What it is:** This was Benjamin Graham's original strategy that Warren Buffett used early in his career. It involves finding a beaten-down, mediocre business trading below its liquidation value. It's like finding a discarded cigar butt on the sidewalk: it's gross, but it gives you "one free puff."
*   **The Munger View:** Munger actively **abandoned and avoided this tier**. He realized that "time is the friend of the wonderful business, and the enemy of the mediocre." With Cigar Butts, you have to constantly sell them after your "free puff," pay taxes, and hunt for new ones. He advised moving away from this completely.

### 🚫 Tier 4: The Value Trap (Too Good to be True)
*   **What it is:** Companies that look unbelievably cheap on paper (e.g., a 25% yield or a P/E of 3) but are actually cheap because they are marching toward bankruptcy, carrying fatal amounts of debt, or are in a dying industry.
*   **The Munger View:** Run away. A bad business will eventually destroy your capital, no matter how cheap the stock currently is.
*   **In our project:** This is exactly why we hard-capped our algorithm at a 15% yield—to strictly block these traps from ever reaching your radar.
